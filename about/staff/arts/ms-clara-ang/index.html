Ms Clara Ang

I graduated with a Bachelor of Social Sciences (Second Upper Honours) in Geography from the National University of Singapore. During my undergraduate years, I had the opportunity to be enrolled in the University Scholars Programme (USP), as well as to undertake a six-month exchange at the University of Southampton, UK. My interest in research led me to undertake my postgraduate education, where I obtained my Masters in Social Sciences (Geography) with a dissertation on the Emotional Geographies of Education in Singapore. More recently, I graduated from the National Institute of Education with a Postgraduate Diploma in Education.

My teaching journey began when I signed up for a teaching internship right after the A-Levels, after which I joined a private educational firm where I could visit different schools and be exposed to the cultures of schools across Singapore. I also served as a tutor when I was a postgraduate student, where I taught three different geography undergraduate modules. These experiences allowed me to interact with many excellent educators, who inspired me through their passion and sense of hopefulness, as well as students from a variety of backgrounds and age-groups, who inspired me with their resilience and sense of wonder. Such encounters were crucial in playing a part in shaping my teaching philosophies, which are underpinned by an ethical commitment to use Geography and education as platforms to forge fairer and kinder societies.

Prior to joining EJC, I taught Geography and Literature in English in a secondary school for three years, where I was also a teacher-in-charge of the Drama Club. Teaching students across all abilities, streams, and levels was challenging. However, navigating my students’ complex lifeworlds broadened and deepened my understanding of the multiplicities and complexities of the roles of education. It also taught me the importance of humility as a teacher, and putting students at the heart of everything I do in my job. To this end, I acknowledge that my teaching practice is always a work-in-progress, shifting and adapting to the ever-changing world we live in.

I look forward to joining the huge Eunoia family, working alongside fellow educators and students to craft beautiful thinking, and to spread goodwill to all.
