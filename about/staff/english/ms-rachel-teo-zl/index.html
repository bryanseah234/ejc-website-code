Ms Rachel Teo

I read English Literature at Nanyang Technological University and graduated with a Bachelor of Arts (Second Upper Honours). My journey as an undergraduate was a life-altering one. A new-found interest in postmodern philosophy in 20th Century American Literature led me to perceive the world through renewed, critical lenses. The analytical and interpretative reading of and writing about literary texts also deepened my love for the English Language. In 2011, I was one of 12 teachers, whose stories were selected in a story-writing competition organised by the Ministry of Education. This compilation of stories has been used in the English Language Curriculum in Primary Schools since 2013. In 2018, I also co-authored a Study Companion for a Singapore GCE 'O' Level Literature text, which was published by Ethos Books.

After completing a Postgraduate Diploma in Education at the National Institute of Education in 2011, I began my teaching career at a junior college teaching both English Literature and General Paper (GP). My personal and professional growth as a GP teacher has developed a passion for the subject that has surpassed even my love for literature. Both GP and Literature engage with humanistic concepts such as ideology, relationships and empathy. Besides fostering an appreciation for language and knowledge, GP accentuates our awareness of the human condition, while taking into consideration current world phenomena and universal values that we all share. I believe that language and knowledge are tools of empowerment, and their impact is profoundly connected to the context in which they are wielded. Hence, my goal as a GP teacher is to embolden students to interrogate their experience in the world today. In an increasingly diverse and ambiguous reality, students must be guided by a heart of empathy, courage and responsibility to etch a voice of their own, while entertaining alternative perspectives.

Outside of teaching, I enjoy travelling and reading. I try to constantly expose myself to new experiences and be open to new perspectives so as to challenge my own. I believe that doing so enriches the mind and prevents the mind from being too comfortable and complacent.

It is my honour to belong to a teaching community in Eunoia that prides itself on nurturing compassionate leaders to serve community and nation, and engendering a spirit of life-long learning in every Eunoian.
