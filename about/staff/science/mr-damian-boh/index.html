Mr Damian Boh

Under the A*STAR National Science Scholarship, I graduated from Imperial College London in 2015 with a Bachelor of Science (First Class Honours) in Physics with Theoretical Physics. In the same year, I was awarded the A*STAR Roll of Honour for being one of the most outstanding scholars. In 2013, I also received the German Academic Exchange Service Scholarship to do research at the Max Planck Institute for the Structure and Dynamics of Matter in Germany, where I worked with ultrafast electron diffraction.

Before joining the teaching profession, I worked in several A*STAR research institutes in areas within microelectronics, data storage and robotics. I also joined A*STAR’s ambassador programme, actively volunteering in science outreach activities and attending seminars to learn more about science communication to the public. I have hosted visits to A*STAR research institutes for both local and foreign students. I have also attended forum sessions to share my research with JC and secondary school A*STAR science awardees and participants of the A*STAR Research Exposure Programme. In particular, I was one of the judges and question setters in the National Science Challenge 2016, a nationwide competitive televised game show for secondary school students.

Under the A*STAR-MOE Scientists-in-School programme, I was attached to an integrated programme secondary school and JC where I taught programming and shared its various applications in simulations, robotics and microcontrollers. These experiences complement a teaching stint in an integrated programme school in 2014, where I taught the first batch of students in its newly-implemented science talent programme and trained students for the Singapore Junior Physics Olympiad.

My science outreach and teaching experiences exposed me to the immeasurable joy of imparting knowledge and spreading my passion in science to students. This resulted in my decision to give up several overseas PhD offers and switch from research to full-time teaching, pursuing a career which I find extremely fulfilling and purposeful. Through sharing about my personal research experiences, I hope to inspire Eunoians to see the authenticity of physics, its impressive real-world applications and immense potential in driving future technological advances in various fields. I am deeply intrigued by the overarching goal of physics to unify all the complexity in this universe into a simple, coherent framework. I aim to adopt a similar approach in teaching to enable Eunoians to appreciate the beauty of the underlying connections between seemingly disparate topics and physical phenomena in their everyday lives.

My hobbies include programming, 3D-printing and DIY electronics. I enjoy building mini-robots, gadgets, retro game consoles and Internet of Things (IoT) applications with the Arduino microcontroller and Raspberry Pi. I am fully supportive of the emerging open-source prototyping platforms and the growing Maker Movement in Singapore, and I aim to expose students to these to unleash our new generation’s inventive spirit and innovation. I also enjoy coding websites and developing mobile applications. I am motivated to use my hobbies to develop new teaching tools. Finally, I enjoy music and coming up with creative ways of playing different instruments together.
